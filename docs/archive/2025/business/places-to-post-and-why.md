## Overview

Strategic posting locations for floyo launch and growth, including Reddit, Indie Hackers, Product Hunt, LinkedIn, and niche communities.

**Posting Strategy:** Value-first, no spam, community engagement  
**Posting Cadence:** Weekly (Reddit), Daily (Twitter/X), Bi-weekly (LinkedIn)

## Primary Channels

### 1. Reddit

**Subreddits:**

**r/productivity** (2.5M members)
- **Post Angle:** "Save 5-10 hours/week automating workflows"
- **Post Type:** Tutorial, case study, value-first
- **Cadence:** Weekly (if valuable content)
- **Why:** Target audience (productivity-focused users)

**r/automation** (150K members)
- **Post Angle:** "Local-first workflow automation"
- **Post Type:** Technical tutorial, integration examples
- **Cadence:** Weekly (if valuable content)
- **Why:** Technical audience, automation enthusiasts

**r/indiebiz** (50K members)
- **Post Angle:** "Bootstrapped workflow automation for $12 CAD/month"
- **Post Type:** Build in public, launch announcement
- **Cadence:** Weekly (if valuable content)
- **Why:** Solo operators, bootstrapped founders

**r/SideProject** (100K members)
- **Post Angle:** "Local-first workflow automation side project"
- **Post Type:** Launch announcement, progress updates
- **Cadence:** Weekly (if valuable content)
- **Why:** Side project community, early adopters

**Posting Guidelines:**
- **Value-First:** Provide value (tutorials, case studies)
- **No Spam:** Don't spam, engage authentically
- **Community Rules:** Follow subreddit rules (read rules first)
- **Engagement:** Respond to comments, build relationships

- **Mitigation:** Value-first content, optimal timing

### Risk 2: Community Backlash
- **Mitigation:** Follow community rules, engage authentically
- **Contingency:** Apologize, learn, improve

### Risk 3: Spam Filters
- **Mitigation:** Value-first content, no spam
- **Contingency:** Adjust posting strategy
